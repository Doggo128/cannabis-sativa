WHAT IS CANNABIS?

Cannabis Sativa is an annual flowering plant, native to Eastern Asia. It has been cultivated throughout history for various purposes including:

  • High strength industrial fiber
  • Hemp Seed Oil (lubricants, soaps, food, biodiesel, plastics, etc)
  • Food
  • Medicine (anxiety, sleep, pain relief, etc)
  • Recreation

Cannabis plants have a plethora of uses besides being used to “get high”.

Cannabis plants contain molecules called “Cannabinoids”. These cannabinoids are what interact with the Endocannabinoid System found in most mammals. The most popular cannabinoid is “delta-9-tetrahydrocannabinol” (delta-9-THC); This cannabinoid is responsible for the psychoactive effects produced from smoking the flowers of the plant.
